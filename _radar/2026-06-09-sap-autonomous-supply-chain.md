SAP's supply chain signal frames agentic AI as a structural shift, not a tool upgrade. The operating model changes when agents make decisions that previously required human judgment — demand sensing, exception handling, and supplier rerouting.

The AMS angle is guardrails. Before any rollout, validate: data-foundation quality, process ownership boundaries, agent approval gates, and edge-case output testing. An AI without boundaries is a liability with a fast response time.

What matters is whether the change produces measurable outcomes: fewer manual escalations, faster exception resolution, or reduced supplier downtime. The test is whether the new model delivers operational results that justify the implementation risk, not whether the technology is new.
