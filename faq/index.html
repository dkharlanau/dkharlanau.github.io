FAQ

Answering high-frequency SAP O2C objections

When stakeholders question AMS value, platform choices, or AI enablement, these responses make the economics and guardrails clear.

How do you reduce O2C incidents if SLAs are already green?

We pivot from patching to prevention: build a KEDB, codify playbooks, and wire observability packs across interfaces. With backlog age, repeat-rate, and MTTR tracked weekly, we cut noise and redeploy capacity into automation.

Do you force SAP BTP for every extension?

No. Clean core S/4HANA stays sacred, but the edge remains composable. We adopt BTP when it clearly wins on capability or economics; otherwise, we run open runtimes with portable contracts so exit costs stay low.

How do you keep AI/ML initiatives auditable around SAP?

AI lives side-by-side: retrieval-first patterns, feature stores, and human-in-loop approvals. We log prompts, datasets, and retention rules so compliance trusts the result and operators keep full control.
