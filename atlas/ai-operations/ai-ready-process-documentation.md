<h1>AI-ready process documentation</h1>

<p class="note-subtitle">AI-assisted support improves when process knowledge is structured, current, scoped, and tied to evidence instead of scattered across chats and old handovers.</p>

<div class="note-body">

<h2>Where this fits</h2>

<p>This page connects operational memory, runbooks, knowledge-base design, and AI retrieval. It is useful before building a support copilot or ticket assistant.</p>

<h2>Common issues</h2>

<ul>

<li>Documentation describes ideal process flow but not exceptions, ownership, diagnostic evidence, or escalation boundaries.</li>

<li>Runbooks are stale, unversioned, and not linked to system context or common symptoms.</li>

<li>AI retrieval finds the wrong content because documents lack clear scope and metadata.</li>

</ul>

<h2>Diagnostic questions</h2>

<ul>

<li>Does each process note say where it applies, who owns it, and when it was last reviewed?</li>

<li>Are exceptions and support symptoms documented with evidence requirements?</li>

<li>Can a reader or retrieval system distinguish official process from draft notes?</li>

</ul>
