  <h1>Use AI safely and effectively for professional analysis work.</h1>
  <p class="lead">Skills for validating AI-generated output, converting unstructured notes into structured requirements, decomposing business problems into actionable backlogs, and defining accountability so AI assistance does not become blame avoidance. Designed for enterprise, SAP, and data projects.</p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <header class="section-heading">
    <h2>What this group covers</h2>
  </header>
  <p>AI-Assisted Analysis skills help you work with AI without losing control. They provide structured methods for catching AI hallucinations, preserving nuance when AI structures information, turning AI-generated ideas into executable backlogs, and defining who is responsible when AI output goes wrong.</p>
  <p>These skills are cross-domain. They apply when you use AI to draft requirements, analyze tickets, decompose problems, or produce client-facing documents. Each skill page includes a working method, decision rules, artifact templates, quality checklists, and specific instructions for AI agents.</p>
  <p>The focus is on operational workflows, not on how AI works conceptually. These pages assume you already use AI tools and need to make that use reliable, accountable, and productive.</p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <header class="section-heading">
    <h2>When to use this group</h2>
  </header>
  <ul>
    <li>You have used AI to generate requirements and need to check them before sending them to stakeholders.</li>
    <li>You have raw workshop notes, transcripts, or ticket threads and want AI to help structure them without losing detail.</li>
    <li>A business complaint arrives as a vague statement and you need AI to help decompose it into an analysis backlog.</li>
    <li>Your team uses AI tools and you need a governance framework for ownership, review, and error handling.</li>
    <li>An AI-generated error has reached a client and you need to define what happens next.</li>
    <li>You want to distinguish AI assistance from AI delegation and set clear boundaries for what AI can decide.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <header class="section-heading">
    <h2>Status and limitations</h2>
  </header>
  <p>This is a public working interpretation of AI-assisted analysis practices. It is not official AI vendor documentation, SAP guidance, or BABOK framework content. These skills are designed for practical use on real projects where AI tools are already in use.</p>
  <p>AI tools change rapidly; the hallucination patterns and validation techniques described here are representative, not exhaustive. The accountability framework must be adapted to local laws, contractual terms, and organizational policies. Every skill page includes a verification status and limitations section. Use these skills as structured starting points, not as authoritative framework substitutes.</p>
