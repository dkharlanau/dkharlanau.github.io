  <h1>Turn work into reusable knowledge.</h1>
  <p class="lead">Skills for writing handover notes, decision summaries, action logs, process documentation, incident records, knowledge articles, runbooks, status updates, and lessons learned. Every skill produces a concrete document that the next person can read and act on.</p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <header class="section-heading">
    <h2>What this group covers</h2>
  </header>
  <p>Work Documentation and Handover skills help you convert messy, incomplete, or tribal knowledge into structured artifacts that survive staff changes, time zones, and project phases. These skills cover the full documentation lifecycle: from capturing a decision in a meeting to writing a runbook that an on-call engineer can follow at 2 a.m.</p>
  <p>These skills are cross-domain. They apply to SAP projects, integration teams, data governance initiatives, operational support, and consulting engagements. They are designed to be used together: meeting notes feed action logs, action logs feed decision summaries, and decision summaries feed handover packages.</p>
  <p>Each skill page includes: a working method, diagnostic questions, decision rules, artifact templates, quality checklists, and specific instructions for AI agents.</p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <header class="section-heading">
    <h2>When to use this group</h2>
  </header>
  <ul>
    <li>A project is ending and the team must hand over knowledge to operations or the next phase.</li>
    <li>A decision was made in a meeting and the team needs a record of what was decided, why, and what happens next.</li>
    <li>A meeting produced notes but no one knows who is doing what by when.</li>
    <li>A process exists only in someone's head and the team needs to document it before they leave or go on vacation.</li>
    <li>An incident was resolved and the team needs to capture what happened so the next incident is handled faster.</li>
    <li>A recurring question or fix needs to be turned into a self-service knowledge article.</li>
    <li>An operational procedure needs a step-by-step runbook that a new team member can follow safely.</li>
    <li>A steering committee or sponsor needs a status update that is honest, structured, and actionable.</li>
    <li>A project or phase has ended and the team needs to capture what worked, what did not, and what to change next time.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
