<h2>The last extension for classical ERP functionality inside S/4HANA ends in May 2026</h2>

<p>SAP announced a final five-month transition period for compatibility packs, moving the expiration from December 31, 2025 to the end of May 2026. Compatibility packs allow certain classical SAP ERP functionalities to operate within S/4HANA on-premise, easing the migration path from ECC.</p>

<p>For operations teams, this means three things:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Immediate audit required.</strong> Any S/4HANA on-premise system still relying on compatibility packs now has a hard deadline. After May 2026, those functionalities will no longer have usage rights.</li>
  <li><strong>Cloud solution mapping.</strong> SAP is offering tailored programs for customers moving to the SAP cloud solutions that replace compatibility pack functionalities. The specific replacement products are documented in SAP Note 2269324.</li>
  <li><strong>Support scope change.</strong> Post-May 2026, support incidents related to compatibility pack functionality may be classified as out-of-scope or require a separate engagement.</li>
</ul>

<p>SAP describes this as a final transition period, not a recurring extension. The communication explicitly states this is the last extension to provide "greater choice and flexibility" for customers who need additional time.</p>

<p>For AMS providers, this is a concrete deadline to include in customer QBRs and upgrade planning conversations. The question is no longer "when should we migrate?" but "which replacement functionality is already available and tested?"</p>
