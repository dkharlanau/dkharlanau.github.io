<h2>Joule is no longer a side feature — it is now embedded in core S/4HANA operations</h2>

<p>SAP's Q1 2026 Business AI release expands Joule from a conversational interface into an operational layer. The headline numbers: 35 solutions, 30+ specialized agents, and 2,500+ Joule Skills. For AMS and operations teams, the practical shift is that AI is now touching transactional workflows, not just self-service queries.</p>

<p>Three agents matter most for S/4HANA support:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Project Setup Agent</strong> (S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition) — draws on historical project data to reduce creation time by 10% and resource allocation time by 16%.</li>
  <li><strong>Dispute Resolution Agent</strong> (S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition, beta) — scans invoices, sales orders, delivery records, and pricing agreements to automate root-cause analysis for AR disputes.</li>
  <li><strong>Sales Order Creation from Unstructured Data</strong> (GA) — extracts data from PDF or image-based purchase orders via SAP Document AI and proposes sales order requests.</li>
</ul>

<p>Payment advice processing also went GA, with SAP Document AI extracting amounts, references, and currencies across languages. SAP claims 70% reduction in document processing time and 83% reduction in template maintenance.</p>

<p>For operations teams, the watch item is not the technology but the governance: as agents start creating and modifying transactional data, the boundary between "AI-assisted" and "AI-authorized" becomes a support incident category.</p>
