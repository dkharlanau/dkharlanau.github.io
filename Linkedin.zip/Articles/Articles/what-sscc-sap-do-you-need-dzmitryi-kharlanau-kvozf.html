What is SSCC in SAP and Do You Need It?

Created on 2024-09-25 21:02

Published on 2024-09-25 21:03

SSCC stands for Serial Shipping Container Code, a unique 18-digit number used to track logistics units like pallets or containers. It’s part of the GS1 standard, making it useful for tracking shipments through the entire supply chain.

Do You Need SSCC?

Using SSCC in SAP isn’t mandatory, but here’s when it comes in handy:

  • Simple Operations? If your logistics are mostly internal or simple, you might not need SSCC. You can get by with standard SAP numbers like delivery or handling unit numbers.

  • Global Supply Chains? If you’re shipping internationally or dealing with complex logistics, SSCC is a lifesaver. It helps track shipments across different systems, especially when your partners require it.

  • Industry Regulations? In industries like pharma, automotive, and food, SSCC might be required for tracking and compliance. It ensures you meet traceability standards.

  • Warehouse Automation? Using Warehouse Management (WM) or Extended Warehouse Management (EWM)? SSCC makes goods movements smoother and more automated by integrating with barcode scanning and tracking.

How Do You Generate SSCC in SAP?

  1. Automatic Generation: SAP can automatically generate SSCCs from predefined number ranges during handling unit creation, packing, or shipping.

  2. Manual Entry: You can also manually assign SSCCs if needed, especially if they’re provided by a third party.

  3. Barcode Integration: SSCCs are often printed on shipping labels, making them easy to scan and track through the supply chain.

Why SSCC is Useful

  • Global Standard: It’s a recognized global identifier, so everyone across the supply chain knows what it is.

  • Improved Tracking: It helps you trace every logistics unit from the warehouse to the customer.

  • Automation: SSCCs streamline shipping and receiving processes, especially when using barcode scanners.

Understanding ILN Base Number:
  • The ILN (International Location Number) is a 13-digit number issued by national subsidiaries of EAN International.
  • In SSCC generation, the first seven digits of the ILN Type 2 form the base number.
  • This ILN base number is then used as part of the SSCC and HU (Handling Unit) number generation process.
  • If this base number is missing or incorrect in the configuration, the SSCC generation will fail.
