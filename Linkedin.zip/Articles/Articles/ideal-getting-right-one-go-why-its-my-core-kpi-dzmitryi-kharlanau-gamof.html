The Ideal: Getting It Right in One Go (Why It’s My Core KPI)

Created on 2024-10-01 13:50

Published on 2024-10-02 18:00

In SAP projects, I’ve seen it all—iterations, reworks, testing, and fixes. But what really stands out to me as the gold standard is when things are done right the first time.

I’ve watched some of my colleagues nail their work in one attempt, and honestly, that’s the kind of result I strive for. It’s become a core KPI (Key Performance Indicator).

Why One Attempt is the Ultimate Goal

Sure, iterations are part of the process, but let’s be real: the best-case scenario is when you don’t need multiple rounds. When you can deliver quality, accuracy, and performance in one go, you’re saving time, avoiding unnecessary testing, and most importantly, exceeding expectations right out of the gate.

The Best I've Seen

I’ve observed some of the best developers get it right in one attempt, and it’s impressive. No endless cycles of tweaking or debugging. They hit the mark immediately, and that’s what I consider the pinnacle of professional efficiency. Achieving that level of precision on the first try isn’t easy, but it’s possible.

My KPI: How I Measure Success

For me, the KPI is simple: How close can I get to perfection in the first attempt?

  • Nail it in one: That’s the dream—when everything just works. No need for further iterations.

  • Minor adjustments: If I only need small tweaks, I’m still on track.

  • Multiple iterations: This happens, but it means something went wrong in the early stages. Time to reevaluate.

How to Achieve It

Here’s how I approach getting things right the first time:

  1. The more detailed the plan, the fewer surprises during development. I spend time upfront thinking through potential issues.

  2. Before going all-in, I run quick tests to confirm that the main logic works. This saves me from having to backtrack later.

  3. Checking my own work as I go, making sure every piece fits before moving on to the next part. It’s a simple habit that pays off.
