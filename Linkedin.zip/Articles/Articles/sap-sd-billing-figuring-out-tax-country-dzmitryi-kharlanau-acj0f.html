Getting the right countries for tax purposes isn’t always straightforward, and cross-company situations can complicate things even more. But no worries — we’re here to simplify it for you.

Case 1: Customer Billing Documents

What’s the deal? Normally, when SAP does its tax thing, it looks at:

  • The country where the goods are coming from (the supplying plant country).

  • The country where the goods are going to (the ship-to party country).

You’ll use the customer master and material master data to grab the right tax classifications based on that departure country. Then, you hit up the tax condition records using both the departure and destination countries. Simple, right?

But here’s the kicker: If you're in a cross-company situation, you might want to base your departure country on the sales organization’s country, not the plant’s country. SAP doesn’t do this automatically, so you got tweak things a bit.
