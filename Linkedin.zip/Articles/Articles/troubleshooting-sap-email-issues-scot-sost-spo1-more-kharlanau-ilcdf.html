Troubleshooting SAP Email Issues: SCOT, SOST, SPO1, and More

Ever sent an email in SAP only to see it stuck in SOST, wonder why the SCOT node isn’t green, or scratch your head over SMTP errors? If you’ve ever struggled with SAP email issues, you know how frustrating it can be when things just don’t work. Is it the SMTP configuration? The mail server? The job not running? Or something completely random?

Let’s cut through the noise. Here’s a guide to troubleshoot SAP email issues step-by-step, so you can figure out what’s wrong and get those emails flowing again.

Step 1: Is SCOT Set Up Correctly?

Start with the basics: SCOT is the heart of SAP email communication.

  • Open transaction SCOT.

  • Check if the SMTP node is green.If red: Double-check the Mail Host and Port.Example: SMTP Host: | Port: (or for secure mail).If yellow: The node might be misconfigured or delayed.

Quick Check:

  • Click on the node → Verify the authentication credentials. Are the email server username and password correct?

Step 2: Can SAP Talk to the SMTP Server?

Even if SCOT looks good, SAP might not be able to reach the mail server.

  • Go to SMICM.Navigate to Goto → Services and check if the SMTP service is running.

Step 3: What’s Happening in SOST?

SOST is your go-to transaction to check email statuses:

  • Open SOST.

  • Emails with red or yellow statuses indicate issues.

Common Issues:

  • Red: Authentication errors, invalid recipients, or server rejection.

  • Yellow: Emails are queued but not processed.Ensure the job RSCONN01 is running.

Step 4: Debugging SCOT and SMTP Errors

If SMTP isn’t playing nice, dive into the logs:

  • Go to SCOT → Utilities → Trace → Display Trace.

  • Use SLG1 to search for SAPconnect logs for more details.

Common Errors:

  • 550 Relay Not Permitted: Mail server rejects your connection. Fix: Allowlist your SAP system’s IP on the SMTP server.

  • 503 Bad Sequence of Commands: Something’s off in SCOT’s configuration. Recheck credentials and sequence settings.

Step 5: Are Spool Jobs Holding You Back?

If emails are triggered by print or spool jobs, check SPO1:

  • Open SPO1.

  • Look for spool requests tied to your emails.Error in Spool: Fix the output device in SPAD or check the document formatting (e.g., PDF issues).

Step 6: Key Jobs and Delays

For SAP emails to flow smoothly, key background jobs need to run:

  • RSCONN01: Processes email queues. Ensure this job runs periodically (e.g., every 5 minutes).

  • SOST Cleanup: Old or failed emails can clog the queue. Regularly clean them up.

Why Does This Happen?

SAP email issues usually stem from:

  • Misconfigured SCOT settings.

  • Network issues blocking the SMTP server.

  • Forgotten background jobs (e.g., RSCONN01).

  • Incorrect recipient or formatting errors.
