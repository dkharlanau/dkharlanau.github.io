SAP Lifehack: Don’t Skip Regression Testing!

Created on 2024-10-07 10:44

Published on 2024-10-07 18:15

When working on SAP projects, it’s easy to focus on the shiny new change you’re making. But here’s the deal—while you’re busy testing the new stuff, you also need to make sure you haven’t broken anything that was already working. That’s what regression testing is all about.

Why It’s Important

Every tweak in SAP has the potential to mess with other parts of the system. You can’t just assume everything else will keep running smoothly. Sure, the new feature might work, but have you checked if the old processes are still doing their job? If not, you could be in for a nasty surprise.

My Experience

I once made a small tweak to pricing logic. It worked perfectly in the new transactions. But after we went live, we found out that older transactions were getting messed up. Why? We didn’t test them! Proper regression testing would have caught that.

It Saves Headaches

Running these tests might feel like extra work, but it’s worth it. Catching an issue during testing is way easier than dealing with the fallout once it’s live.

Bottom Line: Test It All

Always test both the new changes and the old behavior. Make sure nothing breaks. It’s simple, but it’ll save you a lot of stress down the road.
