How to Organize Customer-Specific Customizing and Maintenance Objects

Created on 2024-12-13 11:23

Published on 2024-12-26 20:30

Managing customer-specific customizing and maintenance objects can become a real headache as projects evolve. I’ve learned that deciding on a solid structure at the start of a project can save you a ton of time and effort later. Here’s what works for me when organizing these types of data:

Use SPRO for Customizing Entries

SPRO is an excellent starting point for structuring and documenting your customizations. I typically use S_IMG_EXTENSION to create an IMG node for my custom objects. A couple of things to keep in mind:

  • SAP keeps custom entries separate, and after upgrades, you can merge them back.

  • You can enhance structures and add documentation to make your configuration user-friendly and future-proof.

Group Data with SE43

If I’m working with master data or related transactions, I use SE43 to create Area Menus. It helps me group everything logically, making it easier to find related items.

Create Process-Guided Transactions

Sometimes, I need a tailored approach to maintain interlinked customizing. I’ll write a custom program or design a Fiori app with process flows to ensure consistency across the setup.

Link and Store Data in Custom Tables

For tracking activations, constants, or change requests, I create a central table. This ensures traceability and makes future debugging easier.

Leverage TVARVC or SETLEAF for Simplifying Constants

TVARVC is my go-to when I need to store parameters or selection options to avoid hardcoding. For more structured data, I use SETLEAF because it validates entries against the domain values and is easier to manage with transactions GS01/GS02/GS03.

Document Everything in a Wiki

Creating a shared wiki with links to custom objects, configurations, and documentation helps the entire team stay aligned. It’s especially useful for onboarding new team members.

Use BRF+ for Function-Dependent Customizing

When I need a dynamic customizing approach, BRF+ lets me manage function-dependent variables in context. It’s flexible and integrates well with SAP.

Manage Constants in Declared Interfaces
For technical constants, I prefer interfaces in SE24. This approach keeps my code clean and centralized, especially for reusable configurations.
Use External Services for Business Rules
Sometimes, storing rules outside SAP makes sense. External Business Rule Management tools can handle function-specific logic, ensuring separation of concerns.
Tips for Maintenance
  • Regularly review and update documentation.
  • Use archiving to remove obsolete entries.
  • Ensure new entries follow naming conventions and structural guidelines.
