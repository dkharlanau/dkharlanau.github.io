Intrastat in SAP: How It’s Managed

Created on 2024-10-02 07:26

Published on 2024-10-05 16:30

If you’re dealing with goods moving across borders within the EU, Intrastat reporting is something you need to get right. SAP helps you automate this process by pulling the necessary data directly from your system, reducing manual work. Here’s how Intrastat declarations work in SAP, step by step, along with key features.

Key Features of Intrastat in SAP

1. Managing Provider Information

Before you can generate Intrastat reports, you need to provide your company’s provider-of-information data. This is required by your country’s statistics authority and needs to be maintained in the system. Think of this as setting up your company’s profile for Intrastat reporting.

2. Creating Intrastat Declarations

Each month, you’ll be required to generate Intrastat reports for:

  • Receipts: Goods your company receives from other EU countries.

  • Dispatches: Goods your company ships to other EU countries.

You can manage these declarations directly in SAP, where the system helps you gather the required statistical data, ensuring all cross-border movements are properly reported.

3. Selecting Dispatches and Customer Returns

For goods that leave your company, SAP selects relevant data from billing documents to generate the Intrastat report. This includes not only standard dispatches but also customer returns—so any goods sent back to you are accounted for.

4. Selecting Receipts and Returns to Suppliers

For incoming goods, SAP pulls data from purchase orders and intercompany billing documents. This data includes receipts and returns to suppliers, ensuring both the goods coming in and going back out are accurately reported.

How It’s Handled in Different EU Countries

SAP has specific setups for handling Intrastat in several countries, including Austria, Italy, Germany, Spain, and many others. Notably, in Italy, the system is also set up to track services, as this is required in addition to goods.

SAP S/4HANA and Intrastat

If you’re using S/4HANA, there’s a shift from the older ERP foreign trade functionality. Here’s what’s different:

  • Foreign trade/customs tabs that existed in ERP are no longer available in S/4HANA.

  • Instead, S/4HANA uses a new international trade approach, with everything now managed through the Fiori apps like Manage Intrastat Declarations or transaction /ECRS/RP_EDIT.

  • Tables like EIKP and EIPO (which used to store data) are no longer updated in S/4HANA. Now, all changes related to Intrastat are made via the new Fiori apps.

Commodity Codes in S/4HANA

  • From S/4HANA release 1909 onwards, you can no longer manage commodity codes through traditional GUI transactions like /SAPSLL/CLSNR_01.

  • Instead, SAP has introduced a Fiori app called Manage Commodity Codes for handling commodity codes. The old method of assigning commodity codes at the material master level has been replaced by apps like Classify Products and Re-Classify Products.

Common Intrastat Challenges and FAQs

  1. If you’re dealing with supplementary units of measure and can’t edit them, this feature has been updated. As of S/4HANA 1809, supplementary units of measure require mapping with customs units.

  2. For Intrastat reporting, the country of origin is usually taken from the batch master data. If no batch exists, it defaults to the material master. As of S/4HANA 2020, this information is also stored at the delivery item level.

  3. In Intrastat receipts, the region is taken from the receiving plant. For dispatches, it checks the region of origin, or, if goods were produced elsewhere, from the delivering plant.

  4. SAP does not allow multiple commodity codes for the same product in one country. However, you can manage end-use functionality for different classifications as needed.

  5. If you’re encountering errors like "You cannot change a declaration with status released," it typically means the job has already processed a declaration for that period (e.g., dispatches). You’ll need to cancel the released status and re-run the job.

Why Use S/4HANA’s International Trade Module?

The International Trade module in S/4HANA offers several benefits over the older foreign trade setup:

  • Better support for more countries (26 countries in S/4HANA vs. 16 in ERP).

  • Streamlined user interface with options for mass updates.

  • Multiple users can work simultaneously, speeding up the reporting process.

  • It reduces data redundancy, as plant-level commodity code assignment is no longer necessary.
